Fair Work Agency

Practice note - w-039-2841 - Last updated 08 Apr 2026 by Sarah Adesina

The Fair Work Agency (FWA) is a single labour-market enforcement body established under Part 5 of the Employment Rights Bill 2024, consolidating functions previously held by existing labour market enforcement bodies.

Remit and powers

The FWA has jurisdiction over national minimum wage enforcement, labour exploitation, and agency worker compliance. Editors update this maintained resource as policy changes are confirmed.

Operational scope

The 2026/27 transitional year establishes implementation priorities, data-sharing expectations, and initial enforcement coordination.

Fair Work Agency

by Practical Law Employment

Maintained • View Resource History • Practice note: overview • England, Scotland, Wales
Opened from Resource Update Alert. One section has been updated since the previous version.

A guide to the Fair Work Agency, including its remit, operational scope, enforcement priorities and relationship with existing labour market enforcement bodies.

Scope of this note

This note explains the purpose and expected operation of the Fair Work Agency and considers the practical implications for employers, workers and advisers.

It tracks the agency's development, including transitional arrangements, strategic priorities and the expected relationship with existing labour market enforcement bodies.

For further background, see Practice note, Employment rights enforcement: overview and Practice note, Labour market enforcement bodies.

Establishment and remit

The Fair Work Agency is expected to bring together labour market enforcement functions and provide a single route for strategic enforcement activity across selected employment rights.

The agency is intended to coordinate enforcement activity across areas including holiday pay, statutory sick pay, umbrella company compliance and labour exploitation.

Operational scope

Updated in this alert
Resource Update Alert context

Updated to reflect the government's strategic steer for the 2026/2027 transitional year, including early focus on intelligence-led enforcement and data sharing.

The FWA's operational scope will initially focus on consolidating intelligence, improving coordination across enforcement routes and preparing the transition from existing labour market enforcement bodies.

The government has indicated that the 2026/2027 transitional year will be used to establish core operating procedures, data sharing arrangements and priority areas for early enforcement activity.

Exact change in Operational scope

The agency is expected to begin work once the relevant commencement provisions are in force. During the 2026/2027 transitional year, the agency is expected to establish core operating procedures, agree data sharing arrangements and prioritise intelligence-led enforcement activity.

The strategic steer also indicates that the FWA should use the transitional year to clarify how it will coordinate with existing labour market enforcement bodies before full operational transfer.

Investigation and enforcement powers

The agency's powers are expected to build on existing enforcement mechanisms, including the ability to investigate suspected non-compliance and coordinate enforcement activity across multiple employment rights.

Further detail is expected as the relevant commencement provisions and operational guidance are published.

Data sharing and intelligence

Data sharing is expected to be central to the agency's approach, allowing enforcement bodies to identify repeat issues, target higher-risk sectors and reduce duplication across overlapping enforcement regimes.

For a detailed consideration of information sharing in employment enforcement, see Practice note, Data sharing between labour market enforcement bodies.
